What I work on

I’m most interested in real-world security work that goes beyond theory. That means investigating weird behavior, digging through logs, building better detections, hardening infrastructure, and understanding how attackers actually operate in Windows and Linux environments.

Threat Hunting Playbook Development

One of the projects I’m proud of is developing a modular threat-hunting playbook designed around attacker behavior rather than one-off alerts. The goal was to make investigations faster, more consistent, and easier to scale across different types of suspicious activity.

Detection Engineering

I’ve spent time building and refining dashboards and detections to surface suspicious PowerShell usage, strange login patterns, and potential beaconing activity. I like the process of turning noisy data into something actionable.

Incident Response Mindset

I’m drawn to the technical side of response work: isolating impacted systems, collecting artifacts, tracing activity, and figuring out both what happened and what needs to change so it doesn’t happen again.

How I build

I like projects that let me move between offensive and defensive thinking. That usually means building something functional first, understanding how it can fail, and then improving it with better visibility, stronger configs, and clearer response steps.

01

Start with the system

I like understanding the environment first: how it’s configured, what it’s supposed to do, and what “normal” looks like.

02

Break down behavior

Whether it’s attacker activity, suspicious network traffic, or misconfigured infrastructure, I focus on patterns and root cause instead of just surface symptoms.

03

Turn findings into something useful

The end goal is always something practical: a better detection, a cleaner process, a hardened system, or a stronger prod build.
